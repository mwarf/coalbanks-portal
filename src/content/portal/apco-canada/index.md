## Project Scope

**The 9-1-1 Story: The Evolving Lifeline** — a 120-second documentary piece for APCO Canada, tracing the history of public safety communications in Canada from the 1959 Winnipeg '999' system through to modern NG911.

This is the second video in APCO Canada's series, following the success of Video #1 which premiered at the Niagara conference in November 2025 and was released nationally during Public Safety Telecommunications Week (April 2026).

### Creative Direction

The treatment blends **AI-restored archival footage** with contemporary interviews and operational cinematography, focusing on the people behind the technology — the telecommunicators, dispatchers, and communicators who've kept the lifeline running for over six decades.

The board's direction is clear: **focus on the people doing the work**, not just the technology they use.

### Three-Act Structure

| Act | Era | Focus |
|-----|-----|-------|
| **Act 1 — Origins** | 1959–1980s | Early systems, the '999' switch, first dispatchers |
| **Act 2 — Evolution** | 1990s–2010s | Digital transformation, consolidation, growing complexity |
| **Act 3 — The Lifeline Today** | Present | NG911, PSAP interoperability, the people on the line now |
