## Match-Cut Test Gallery

These images are the first step in building the **Hemsdale match-cut sequence** — the centrepiece of the brand anthem. The concept: open on the Hemsdale at Legacy Park (Lethbridge), then seamlessly transition to the Hemsdale at Coleman (Crowsnest Pass), connecting the urban and mountain sides of Stranville's footprint in a single visual.

### What's in This Gallery

The video above shows early animation tests for the match-cut transition — ranging from simple cuts to more stylised transitions. The simpler versions are likely closer to what will work in the final anthem.

The image set mixes **real photographs** taken today at the Legacy Park Hemsdale showhome with **AI-generated variations** that explore alternate angles, times of day, lighting conditions, and exterior material options. The AI variations were generated from the real reference photos to test whether the match-cut transition can work when the two sides of the cut don't have perfectly matching conditions — different sky colour, slightly different angle, or different exterior finishes.

**Real photos (Legacy Park):**
- Straight-on daytime and twilight exterior shots
- Street-level and aerial (drone) perspectives

**AI variations:**
- Blue hour and twilight lighting from multiple angles
- Alternate exterior materials (cedar siding + river rock vs. standard board-and-batten + ledger stone)
- Mountain setting context for the Coleman side of the cut

### What This Tests

The match-cut needs to feel seamless. These tests help answer:
- Can the Hemsdale floor plan read as the "same home" across two very different locations?
- How much flexibility exists in angle, lighting, and material before the visual continuity breaks?
- Which time of day and angle pairings create the strongest transition?

The results will inform camera placement and scheduling for both Day 1 (Legacy Park) and Day 3 (Coleman Hemsdale) during production week.
